How it works

From manual review to
autonomous intelligence

The system fetches live DIU student data, runs ML predictions, and improves with every semester of new data — with no code changes required.

01
Live DIU API sync Auto
Every 24 hours the system pulls student grades, CGPA, payment status, and counselling records directly from the DIU Mentoring API for all 63 supervised students.
02
ML risk classification
The trained PCA + SVM model classifies each student into Very Low / Low / Medium / High / Very High performance categories in under 1 second per student.
03
Smart alert routing Auto
URGENT students trigger instant email + WhatsApp alerts to the teacher. WATCH students appear on the daily dashboard. Normal students are auto-resolved.
04
Semester retraining Research
Each semester the teacher uploads new survey data. The model retrains on all accumulated data — growing smarter every cycle. This is the core research contribution.
05
Full audit trail
Every prediction, review, and alert is logged with timestamps. The teacher can export the audit trail as CSV directly for the research paper.

Research findings

Five research questions,
five clear answers

Based on real authenticated DIU student data collected over 1.5 years. Target: Q1 journal publication.

Phase 1 · PCA Analysis
Does PCA structure stay stable across dataset sizes?
PCA was applied across N=2k, 5k, and 10k synthetic datasets generated from real student survey data. Cumulative variance and component loadings were compared.
25–26 components needed for 90% variance — stable across all N sizes. Synthetic data is valid.
Phase 2 · Automation Need
At what scale does manual supervision break down?
Manual workload modelled as N grows. Each at-risk student requires 15 minutes of supervisor review time with a 120hr/month capacity per supervisor.
Manual system exceeds capacity at N > 2,000. More than 17 supervisors required. Automation is necessary.
Phase 3 · Agentic vs Manual
How does the agentic system compare to manual review?
Manual supervisor simulated at 75% accuracy (realistic human performance). Agentic AI starts at 46.7% overall accuracy but achieves 69.6% recall on at-risk students.
At-risk recall: +27.1% improvement. 115 extra students helped per 1,000 monitored. Recall, not accuracy, is the right metric.
Phase 4 · Student Benefit
How many more students receive timely help?
Truly at-risk students (Very Low + Low classification) compared between manual detection rate and agentic detection rate across the test set of 1,000 students.
At institution scale (N=10k): ~1,150 extra students receive timely intervention per semester.
Phase 5 · Authority Engagement
How does authority stay engaged without being overwhelmed?
Alert classification routes only actionable cases to the teacher. Full audit trail maintained for every prediction, review, and automated action.
Only 41% require human review. 59% auto-resolved. Complete audit trail with 1,000 logged records.
Main Contribution · Longitudinal
How does the model improve semester by semester?
The core research finding: tracking model accuracy across semesters as cumulative training data grows. Starting point 46.7% is the beginning, not the conclusion.
Accuracy trajectory: 46.7% → 52% → 60% → 67% converging toward faculty baseline of 75%.
